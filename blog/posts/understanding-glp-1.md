# Understanding GLP-1 Medications

GLP-1 receptor agonists have become one of the most effective tools for weight management and type 2 diabetes treatment. Let's explore what they are and how they work.

## What Are GLP-1 Medications?

GLP-1 (Glucagon-Like Peptide-1) is a hormone produced in the gut that helps regulate blood sugar and appetite. GLP-1 medications are synthetic versions of this hormone.

### Common GLP-1 Medications

- **Ozempic** (semaglutide)
- **Wegovy** (semaglutide)
- **Mounjaro** (tirzepatide)
- **Zepbound** (tirzepatide)
- **Trulicity** (dulaglutide)
- **Victoza** (liraglutide)

## How Do They Work?

GLP-1 medications work in several ways:

### 1. Slowed Gastric Emptying
Food stays in your stomach longer, which:
- Makes you feel full longer
- Reduces appetite
- Leads to reduced calorie intake

### 2. Insulin Secretion
When blood sugar is high, these medications:
- Stimulate insulin release from the pancreas
- Reduce glucagon (a hormone that raises blood sugar)

### 3. Appetite Suppression
These medications act on brain receptors that:
- Reduce hunger sensations
- Decrease food cravings
- Help with portion control

## Side Effects

Common side effects include:

- Nausea (most common)
- Vomiting
- Diarrhea
- Constipation
- Stomach pain

> **Important:** Always consult with your healthcare provider before starting or changing any medication regimen.
