# Why Privacy Matters in Health Apps

Your health data is personal. It reveals intimate details about your life, habits, and medical conditions. Here's why we believe privacy should be a priority in health apps.

## The Problem with Most Health Apps

Many popular health apps:

- Require account creation
- Store data in the cloud
- Sell anonymized data to third parties
- Have experienced data breaches
- Track your location and usage

## Our Privacy Philosophy

At GLPal, we believe differently:

### Local Storage Only
All your data stays on your device. We don't have servers that store your information.

### No Accounts Required
No email, no username, no password. Just download and use.

### No Tracking
We don't track your usage, location, or behavior. No analytics, no ads.

### Your Data, Your Control
You can export all your data at any time. It's yours.

## What We Don't Collect

- Personal identification information
- Location data
- Usage analytics
- Health data for research
- Any data that could be sold

## Optional Cloud Backup

For users who want backup options:

- **End-to-end encryption** - We can't read your data
- **You hold the keys** - Only you can access your backup
- **Optional** - It's off by default

## Making Informed Choices

When choosing health apps, ask yourself:

1. What data does this app collect?
2. Where is it stored?
3. Who has access?
4. Can I delete my data?
5. Has there been a data breach?

> Your health data is personal. Choose apps that respect that.
